The Developer's Approach to Volume

In geometry, volume is the 3D space an object occupies. As a developer, I think of volume as a function. Most prisms follow the logic: let volume = areaOfBase * height;. Pyramids simply apply a weight: volume = (1/3) * areaOfBase * height;. Below, we’ll explore the "algorithms" for 15 different solids with full visual representations.

1. Prisms (Base × Height)

Triangular Prism

V = (½bh)L

½(6 × 4) × 10 = 120 units³

Square Prism (Cube)

V = s² × H

5² × 5 = 125 units³

Rectangular Prism

V = l × w × h

8 × 3 × 5 = 120 units³

Trapezoidal Prism

V = [½(a+b)h]L

½(4+6)3 × 10 = 150 units³

Pentagonal Prism

V = BaseArea × H

(27.5) × 10 = 275 units³

Hexagonal Prism

V = BaseArea × H

≈ 41.57 × 10 = 415.7 units³

2. Pyramids (⅓ × Base × Height)

Square Pyramid

V = ⅓s²H

⅓(36) × 10 = 120 units³

Rectangular Pyramid

V = ⅓(lw)H

⅓(20) × 9 = 60 units³

Triangular Pyramid

V = ⅓(Base)H

⅓(12) × 10 = 40 units³

Pentagonal Pyramid

V = ⅓(BaseArea)H

Using Apothem for Base Area

Hexagonal Pyramid

V = ⅓(BaseArea)H

⅓(3√3/2 × s²) × H

3. Cylinders & Cones (π Logic)

Cylinder (r)

V = πr²h

π(3²)10 ≈ 282.7

Cylinder (d)

r = d/2

π(5²)5 ≈ 392.7

Cone (r)

V = ⅓πr²h

⅓π(3²)4 ≈ 37.7

Cone (d)

V = ⅓π(d/2)²h

💻 Developer Corner: Coding the Logic

The JavaScript Logic

We treat each formula as a pure function. This ensures our app is testable and accurate.

// JavaScript Implementation
const calculateVolume = {
  triangularPrism: (b, ht, L) => (0.5 * b * ht) * L,
  cylinder: (r, h) => Math.PI * Math.pow(r, 2) * h,
  cone: (r, h) => (1/3) * Math.PI * Math.pow(r, 2) * h,
  sphere: (r) => (4/3) * Math.PI * Math.pow(r, 3)
};

// UI Example: Input listener
function handleCalc() {
  const r = document.getElementById('radius').value;
  const h = document.getElementById('height').value;
  const result = calculateVolume.cylinder(r, h);
  updateUI(result.toFixed(2));
}
